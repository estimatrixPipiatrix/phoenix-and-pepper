# Decision Philosophy — Phoenix et Piper

## Core Shift

This project evolves from **single-objective optimization (profit)** to **multi-objective decision-making under uncertainty and values**.

> We do not seek the single optimal path.  
> We seek decisions that remain **robust across many plausible futures and value systems**.

---

## The Problem

Classical optimization assumes:
- one objective (e.g., profit)
- stable environment
- complete information

Our world has:
- uncertainty (demand, hazards, divine intervention)
- competing objectives (profit, risk, ethics, reputation)
- emergent system effects (feedback loops, second-order consequences)

---

## Decision Model

Each decision is evaluated across multiple dimensions:

- **Profit**
- **Risk**
- **Ethical Cost**
- **Reputation Impact**
- **Political / Regulatory Risk**

Decisions are no longer scalars — they are **vectors**.

---

## Constraints vs Tradeoffs

### Hard Constraints (Non-negotiable)
- No actions that cause catastrophic harm
- No unsustainable resource extraction
- No enabling clearly destructive actors

These remove options entirely.

---

### Soft Tradeoffs (Negotiable)
- Profit vs risk
- Short-term vs long-term outcomes
- Reputation vs growth

These are handled via:
- weighted preferences
- Pareto-efficient decision sets

---

## Stakeholders

Decisions reflect multiple perspectives:

- **Partners** → profit and growth  
- **Senate** → political stability  
- **Priests** → ethical / divine concerns  
- **Customers** → price vs trust  

> Decisions are negotiated outcomes, not single-objective optimizations.

---

## Systems Thinking

The company operates within a dynamic system:

- Success → expansion → complexity → fragility  
- Risk → losses → conservatism → missed opportunity  
- Ethical shortcuts → instability → long-term loss  

We explicitly model:
- feedback loops  
- delays  
- second-order effects  

---

## Robustness Principle

We adopt a "path integral" mindset:

> Evaluate decisions across many possible futures  
> Prefer strategies that perform **acceptably well across most scenarios**

Avoid:
- brittle optimizations
- overfitting to a single forecast

Prefer:
- graceful degradation
- resilience to shocks

---

## Role of AI

AI is used as:
- analyst (exploration)
- simulator (scenario generation)
- adversary (stress testing decisions)

Humans remain responsible for:
- framing problems
- setting constraints
- evaluating tradeoffs

---

## Guiding Principle

> A good decision is not the one that maximizes profit in one future,  
> but the one that remains **defensible, resilient, and aligned with values across many futures**.
